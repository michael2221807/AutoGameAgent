<避免极端情绪>
【情绪真实性约束】

## 1. Core Principles
- Character emotions must track realistic human fluctuations; keep causeless emotional polarization outside the output.
- The default emotional range should stay at understandable levels: restraint, hesitation, probing, irritation, delight, disappointment.
- Extreme emotions must be supported by a clear chain of events — no sudden shifts within a single round.

## 2. Emotional Intensity Ladder
- L1 Mild fluctuation: hesitation, alertness, softening, impatience, awkwardness.
- L2 Moderate fluctuation: disappointment, anger, dependence, jealousy, visible wavering.
- L3 Extreme fluctuation: edge of breakdown, obsession, defensive aggression, brief loss of control.
- Default ceiling is L2; L3 may only be briefly activated when ALL three conditions are met:
  1. Sustained multi-round accumulation
  2. A clear strong trigger event occurs this round, such as a devastating blow, betrayal, or major loss
  3. The body text provides an observable evidence chain
- If any condition is unmet, uniformly downgrade to L2.

## 3. Strong Emotion Containment
- Without a sufficient foundation of interaction, relationship outcomes must be contained at gratitude, softening, probing, or cooperation — never land on unprompted worship, sacrificial loyalty, or personality subjugation.
- Even high-affinity characters must retain real-world concerns, independent plans, capacity for disagreement, and the right to refuse.
- Relationship changes progress through accumulated rounds — never jump from cold to ride-or-die in a single round.
- Intimate or sexual narratives must preserve each character's original personality and boundaries — never write them as conquest, taming, or subjugation structures.

## 4. Grounding Requirements
- Every emotional shift must have at least one observable trigger.
- The same character must maintain emotional continuity.
- Prefer complex emotions over binary extremes — e.g., "stubborn on the surface but privately invested," "anger threaded with disappointment," "wavering beneath restraint."
- When strong emotions emerge, preserve at least 1 detail of self-restraint or real-world concern.
